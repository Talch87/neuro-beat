NeuroBeat — Results

Live experimental status for the spiking neural network ECG arrhythmia detector.

Current status

NeuroBeat-VEB v1 is shipped: a spiking ventricular-ectopic-beat detector meeting VEB sensitivity ≥ 0.90, VEB PPV ≥ 0.60, and ≤ 25,000 SynOps per beat on the inter-patient MIT-BIH DS1→DS2 split, generalizing across databases. Supraventricular (SVEB) detection is a separate, single-lead-limited problem and is reported as an honest negative result.

A validation-locked, energy-accounted preprint is drafted (source). Its central claim is the evaluation discipline, not accuracy leadership: under an identical protocol, stronger non-spiking baselines match or beat the cascade on accuracy (see below), so the spiking model's case rests on per-beat operation count and neuromorphic suitability, pending measured hardware energy.

Bottom line. The VEB detection product is complete. A two-stage gated-ensemble cascade — a genuinely sparse high-recall screener (about 6,000 SynOps) on every beat, gating a 3-seed ensemble confirmer that runs only on the ~27% of beats it flags — meets all three targets on DS2 simultaneously: sensitivity 0.923, PPV 0.616, 23,385 SynOps/beat.

A single model cannot do this: it faces a real sensitivity–precision tradeoff (about 0.89/0.49 or 0.86/0.68, never both), consistent with calibration and seed variance rather than a capacity limit — a seed ensemble reaches the target corner, and the cascade delivers that ensemble accuracy within the energy budget by running it only on flagged candidates. VEB sensitivity holds ≥ 0.90 across three databases (DS2, SVDB, INCART) under one frozen operating point.

Supraventricular (SVEB) detection is a separate, single-lead-limited problem. A dedicated SVEB specialist (svdb + INCART augmentation, SVEB-only operating point) does not yield a usable detector: DS2 SVEB precision never exceeds ~0.15, high sensitivity comes only from flagging almost everything (and cannibalizes the ventricular class), and it is wildly seed-unstable. The same architecture reaches 0.62 SVEB sensitivity on 12-lead INCART, so the limit is lead count, not capacity — multi-lead input is the natural next lever. This is an honest negative result, and exactly why SVEB must not be allowed to compromise the VEB operating point.

What held up under scrutiny (preprint hardening)

The preprint was hardened with analyses computed from the frozen model (all regenerable, none invented):

Per-record robustness & error composition

Per-record DS2 results show the beat-pooled headline is carried by two large records; one record (213) is genuinely weak (0.62 sensitivity). VEB false positives are 87% normal beats and 12% supraventricular; on the SVEB-dense SVDB, 39% of false positives are true SVEB beats, which is exactly why SVDB PPV drops.

Stronger baselines & honest ranking

Under the identical protocol, a TCN, a ResNet-lite CNN, and gradient-boosted trees all beat the spiking cascade on DS2 F1 (see the table above). The paper states this plainly: the contribution is evaluation discipline and energy accounting, not accuracy leadership.

End-to-end & learning curve

With a standard R-peak detector instead of annotations, end-to-end VEB is 0.884 / 0.593 (below the 0.90 target but a modest drop); a weak detector collapses it. A two-seed training-size sweep is flat, suggesting the limit is not DS1 size — treated as diagnostic, not conclusive.

How the numbers are kept honest

Inter-patient split. The model trains on DS1 patients and is tested on DS2 patients (the de Chazal split). Beats from a test patient never appear in training, so the numbers reflect new patients, not memorized ones.

No test-set tuning. The operating point (per-class decision thresholds) is chosen only on a validation holdout carved out of DS1, then frozen before touching DS2. The test set selects nothing. This is why the reported PPV is lower than tuned numbers seen in some papers, and why it is trustworthy.

Per-class metrics. About 89% of beats are normal, so overall accuracy is easy to inflate. Sensitivity and PPV are reported per class for VEB and SVEB, which is what actually matters for detecting arrhythmias.
